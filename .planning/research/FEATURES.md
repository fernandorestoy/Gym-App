### Chest Exercise Descriptions

**1. Flat Barbell Bench Press**
Lie flat on a bench, grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width, and unrack it over your chest. Lower the bar with control to mid-chest, touching lightly, then press it back up to full lockout. Keep your shoulder blades pinched together and feet flat on the floor throughout.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4Y2ZdHCOXok

**2. Incline Barbell Bench Press**
Set a bench to 30–45 degrees, grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width, and lower it to your upper chest. Press back up powerfully while keeping your elbows at roughly 60–75 degrees from your torso. The incline angle shifts emphasis to the upper (clavicular) head of the pec.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NsEbXsTwas8

**3. Decline Barbell Bench Press**
Set the bench to a 15–30-degree decline and secure your feet under the pads. Lower the bar to your lower chest with control, then press back up to lockout. This angle targets the lower pec and generally allows heavier loads than flat pressing.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LfyQBUKR8SE

**4. Flat Dumbbell Press**
Lie flat on a bench holding a dumbbell in each hand at chest level, palms facing forward. Press both dumbbells up simultaneously until your arms are extended, then lower them slowly with a controlled eccentric. Dumbbells allow a greater range of motion than a barbell and let each arm move independently.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VmB1G1K7v94

**5. Incline Dumbbell Press**
Set a bench to 30–45 degrees and press dumbbells from upper-chest height to full extension overhead. Keep the movement controlled and avoid letting the dumbbells drift forward over your face. This is one of the most effective exercises for upper chest development.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8iPEnn-ltC8

**6. Decline Dumbbell Press**
Secure your feet on a decline bench and hold dumbbells at lower-chest level. Press upward and slightly inward, squeezing the pecs at the top. Lower with control to feel a stretch in the lower chest.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LfyQBUKR8SE

**7. Dumbbell Flat Fly**
Lie flat on a bench with arms extended above your chest, slight bend in the elbows, dumbbells facing each other. Lower the dumbbells in a wide arc until you feel a deep stretch in the pecs, then squeeze them back together at the top. Keep the elbow angle consistent throughout — this is an isolation movement, not a press.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QENKPHhQVi4

**8. Incline Dumbbell Fly**
Set a bench to 30–45 degrees and perform a fly with a wide arc, lowering the dumbbells until the upper chest feels fully stretched. Drive the dumbbells back up and together with a hugging motion, squeezing at the top. The incline shifts emphasis to the upper pec.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0HosW8HpPIQ

**9. Cable Crossover (Low to High)**
Attach handles to the low pulleys of a cable machine and stand in the center. Pull the cables upward and across your body in a wide arc, meeting your hands above your chest. This angle targets the upper and mid pec with continuous tension throughout the movement.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=taI4XduLpTk

**10. Cable Crossover (High to Low)**
Attach handles to the high pulleys and stand in the center with a slight forward lean. Pull the cables downward and across your body, bringing hands together in front of your hips or lower chest. This angle maximally targets the lower pec.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Iwe6AmxVf7o

**11. Seated Cable Pec Fly**
Sit at a cable machine with pulleys set to chest height and hold the handles with a slight elbow bend. Bring your hands together in front of your chest in a wide hugging arc, squeezing hard at the midpoint. This is Jeff Nippard's top-ranked chest exercise for consistent tension and easy progression.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NsEbXsTwas8

**12. Machine Chest Press**
Adjust the seat so the handles are at mid-chest height. Press the handles forward to full arm extension, squeezing the pecs at the end range. The machine provides a stable path that removes balance demands — excellent for focusing on feel and progressive overload.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xnYMEGSU2KA

**13. Machine Fly (Pec Deck)**
Sit at the pec deck machine with forearms resting on the pads. Bring your arms together in a hugging motion until the pads nearly touch, squeezing your pecs hard at the peak. Slowly return to the start for a full stretch. A very safe and effective isolation option.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z57CtFmRMxA

**14. Push-Up**
Start in a high plank with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width. Lower your chest to the floor keeping your body in a straight line from head to heels, elbows at roughly 45 degrees from your torso. Push back up to full arm extension without letting your hips sag.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IODxDxX7oi4

**15. Wide-Grip Push-Up**
Set your hands wider than shoulder-width and perform a standard push-up. The wider grip increases the range of motion for the chest and shifts more of the load to the pecs and away from the triceps. Lower until your chest nearly touches the floor.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IODxDxX7oi4

**16. Diamond Push-Up**
Place your hands close together directly under your chest, forming a diamond shape with thumbs and index fingers. Lower your chest toward your hands, then press back up. This variation heavily loads the triceps and the inner chest.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J0DXDExNScE

**17. Incline Push-Up**
Place your hands on an elevated surface (bench, step, or bar) and perform a push-up. The elevation reduces bodyweight load, making this ideal for beginners building toward a full push-up. Keep your body straight and lower your chest to the surface.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cfns5QiDaHE

**18. Decline Push-Up**
Elevate your feet on a bench or step and place your hands on the floor. Perform a push-up in this angled position, lowering your upper chest toward the floor. The decline shifts emphasis to the upper chest and front deltoid.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SKPab2YC8BE

**19. Archer Push-Up**
Start in a wide push-up stance. As you lower yourself, shift your weight to one arm while the other arm extends to the side nearly straight. Alternate sides each rep. This advanced variation approaches a single-arm push-up and dramatically increases chest loading.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FRn_M4HVfPM

**20. Dips (Chest Focus)**
Grip parallel bars and lean your torso slightly forward. Lower yourself by bending the elbows until you feel a stretch in the chest, then press back up. The forward lean shifts emphasis from triceps to chest — avoid going too deep if you have shoulder issues.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2z8JmcrW-As

**21. Incline Cable Fly**
Set a bench to 30–45 degrees between two low cable pulleys. Hold the handles and, with a slight elbow bend, draw your hands together in an arc above your upper chest. The cable provides constant tension across the full range, which is superior to dumbbells for flies.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=taI4XduLpTk

**22. Single-Arm Cable Fly**
Stand side-on to a cable machine set at chest height. With one arm, perform a fly motion across your body, squeezing the pec at the end range. Single-arm work allows focus on each side independently and increases the range of motion significantly.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Iwe6AmxVf7o

**23. Dumbbell Squeeze Press**
Lie flat on a bench and hold two dumbbells with a neutral grip (palms facing each other). Press them together forcefully throughout the entire press, creating internal rotation of the shoulder and a constant pec contraction. This is an effective variation for the inner chest.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QENKPHhQVi4

**24. Floor Press (Dumbbell)**
Lie on the floor with knees bent holding dumbbells at chest level. Press upward until elbows are nearly locked, then lower until your upper arms rest on the floor briefly. The floor limits range of motion and removes the bottom stretch, making it easier on shoulders.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kHSJwnBPj-Y

**25. Floor Press (Barbell)**
Lie under a rack with a barbell at low height. Unrack and lower the bar until your upper arms touch the floor, pause briefly, then press back up. The floor eliminates leg drive and the bottom position removes shoulder strain — a powerful pressing variation.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPG6wSxGaRA

**26. Close-Grip Bench Press**
Lie flat on a bench and grip the bar with hands shoulder-width apart or slightly narrower. Lower the bar to your chest keeping elbows tucked close to your body. This variation shifts emphasis from the pec to the triceps while still engaging the inner chest.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nEF0bv2FW94

**27. Landmine Press**
Wedge a barbell into a corner or landmine attachment and hold the sleeve end with one or both hands at shoulder level. Press the bar up and away in an arc. The curved path naturally targets the upper chest and is very shoulder-friendly.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ql7FEA9LWfM

**28. Svend Press**
Stand or sit and hold two plates sandwiched between your palms at chest level. Press the plates straight out in front of you while squeezing them together forcefully throughout. Return slowly, maintaining internal tension. A light-weight but intense isolation exercise for inner chest.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RHT3qSxmVAI

**29. Deficit Push-Up**
Elevate your hands on push-up handles, dumbbells, or books so your chest can drop below hand level. Lower all the way into the deficit, getting a deeper chest stretch, then press back up. The extra range of motion at the bottom increases pec stretch and growth stimulus.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9j8X17qSxAI

**30. Plyometric Push-Up**
Perform a push-up but press explosively enough for your hands to leave the floor at the top. Land softly with slightly bent elbows. This power variation builds explosive chest strength and fast-twitch fiber recruitment. Only attempt when standard push-ups are very comfortable.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tivt4eqm5_s

**31. Machine Incline Press**
Adjust the machine seat for an incline press angle and position the handles at upper-chest level. Press to full extension and return slowly for a full stretch. Machines at an incline are excellent for targeting the upper chest with no balance demands.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xnYMEGSU2KA

**32. Loaded Stretch Cable Fly**
Set cable pulleys to the highest position and perform a fly with emphasis on the lowest point of the arc — pause 1–2 seconds at full stretch before pulling back up. This pause at maximum stretch creates a strong hypertrophic stimulus in the pec.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NsEbXsTwas8

**33. Neutral-Grip Dumbbell Press**
Lie flat on a bench with dumbbells held with palms facing each other (neutral grip). Press up and lower with control. This grip reduces internal shoulder rotation stress and works well for those with shoulder discomfort on standard dumbbell press.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VmB1G1K7v94

**34. Banded Push-Up**
Loop a resistance band across your upper back and hold each end under your hands. Perform a standard push-up — the band adds resistance at the top where push-ups are normally easiest. This creates a more linear resistance curve and increases chest activation throughout.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IODxDxX7oi4

### Back Exercise Descriptions

**1. Pull-Up (Overhand)**
Hang from a bar with an overhand grip slightly wider than shoulder-width. Pull your chest toward the bar by driving your elbows down and back, squeezing the lats hard at the top. Lower yourself slowly and fully until arms are completely straight before the next rep.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eGo4IYlbE5g

**2. Chin-Up (Underhand)**
Hang from a bar with a shoulder-width underhand (supinated) grip. Pull your chin above the bar by drawing your elbows down and back, getting a strong lat and bicep contraction at the top. Lower slowly to full arm extension. The underhand grip recruits more bicep than a standard pull-up.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=brhRXlOhsAM

**3. Neutral-Grip Pull-Up**
Hang from parallel handles or a neutral-grip bar with palms facing each other. Pull yourself up until your chin clears the handles, leading with your elbows. This grip is often easier on the elbows and wrists than overhand and provides strong lat activation.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eGo4IYlbE5g

**4. Assisted Pull-Up (Machine)**
Set the counterweight on the assisted pull-up machine to reduce your effective bodyweight. Kneel or stand on the platform and perform pull-ups with either a wide or neutral grip. Ideal for building pulling strength before achieving unassisted reps.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eGo4IYlbE5g

**5. Lat Pulldown (Wide Grip)**
Sit at the lat pulldown machine with your thighs secured under the pad. Grip the bar wider than shoulder-width and pull it down to upper-chest level while leaning back very slightly. Drive your elbows straight down and squeeze your lats at the bottom.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CAwf7n6Luuc

**6. Lat Pulldown (Close Grip)**
Attach a short bar or V-bar to the lat pulldown cable. Pull the handle to your upper chest while keeping your torso mostly upright. The close grip increases the range of lat stretch at the top and targets the lower lats distinctly.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CAwf7n6Luuc

**7. Neutral-Grip Lat Pulldown**
Use a parallel attachment or two single handles on a pulldown cable. Pull both handles to either side of your face while driving your elbows down and in. The neutral grip provides excellent lat stretch and is easy on the shoulder joint.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CAwf7n6Luuc

**8. Single-Arm Lat Pulldown**
Attach a single handle and kneel or sit facing the cable machine. Pull the handle to your chest in an arc while rotating slightly toward the working side. Single-arm work allows greater lat stretch at the top and corrects side-to-side imbalances.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=12xHxUnBEiI

**9. Barbell Bent-Over Row**
Stand with the bar on the floor or in a rack at hip height. Hinge forward until your torso is roughly parallel to the floor. Pull the bar to your lower chest or upper abdomen, driving your elbows back and up. Lower with control and repeat without letting your torso swing excessively.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FWJR5Ve8bnQ

**10. Dumbbell Single-Arm Row**
Place one knee and hand on a bench for support. Let the dumbbell hang from your other arm fully extended toward the floor. Row it up to your hip by driving the elbow straight back, getting a full squeeze at the top. Lower slowly to a full stretch.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pYcpY20QaE8

**11. Chest-Supported Dumbbell Row**
Set a bench to 30–45 degrees and lie face-down with your chest against the pad. Let the dumbbells hang from both arms, then row them up simultaneously, driving elbows back. Chest support eliminates lower-back involvement and momentum — Jeff Nippard ranks this among the best back exercises.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SpJwyAbwDBk

**12. Chest-Supported Machine Row**
Sit at the machine with your chest against the pad. Grip the handles and row them back, squeezing your shoulder blades together hard at the end range. Machine support makes this one of the safest and most effective row variations for pure back hypertrophy.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GZbfZ033f74

**13. Seated Cable Row (Close Grip)**
Sit at the cable row station with your feet on the platform. Pull the close-grip handle to your lower abdomen by driving your elbows back past your torso, squeezing the lats and mid-back. Return the handle forward slowly with a full stretch in the lats.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UCXxvVItLoM

**14. Seated Cable Row (Wide Grip)**
Use a wide bar attachment on the cable row. Pull to your upper abdomen with a wider elbow flare, which shifts emphasis more to the upper back and rear delts. Keep your torso still throughout.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UCXxvVItLoM

**15. T-Bar Row**
Straddle the barbell with one end wedged in a corner (or use a T-bar machine). Grip the bar with a neutral or overhand grip and row it to your chest. Keep your back flat and torso at a 45-degree angle. This heavy compound row builds thick mid-back and lat mass.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j3Ux5JOQEYE

**16. Pendlay Row**
Place a barbell on the floor. Hinge to a position with your back completely parallel to the floor and pull the bar explosively from the floor to your chest each rep, returning it fully to the floor before the next. This strict reset eliminates momentum and builds raw pulling power.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Weu9HMHdiDA

**17. Incline Dumbbell Row**
Set a bench to a 30-degree incline and lie face-down. Let dumbbells hang from your arms and row them up toward your hips. The slight forward incline gives your lats a longer stretch than a flat bench row.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pYcpY20QaE8

**18. Meadows Row**
Stand perpendicular to a landmine barbell. Grip the sleeve end and row the barbell to your hip in a powerful one-arm movement. The landmine angle creates a unique arc of resistance that heavily loads the lat at the bottom stretch position.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN6yPtKH5WA

**19. Straight-Arm Pulldown**
Stand facing a high cable pulley with a straight bar or rope attachment. With arms nearly straight (slight elbow bend), pull the bar down in a wide arc from overhead to your thighs. This isolates the lat without significant bicep involvement — excellent for lat mind-muscle connection.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oeGsLVLGNvg

**20. Dumbbell Lat Pullover**
Lie perpendicular across a bench with your upper back supported. Hold one dumbbell with both hands above your chest. Lower it behind your head in a wide arc until you feel a deep lat and serratus stretch, then pull it back over your chest.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FK4rHfGqFJo

**21. Cable Pullover**
Attach a rope to a high pulley and kneel facing the machine. Pull the rope down and forward in an arc, bringing your hands toward your thighs. The cable maintains constant tension throughout the arc unlike a dumbbell — highly effective for lat isolation.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oeGsLVLGNvg

**22. Face Pull**
Attach a rope to a high cable pulley at face height. Pull the rope toward your face, splitting the rope ends to either side of your head while flaring your elbows out. This trains the rear delts and upper traps/rhomboids and is essential for shoulder health.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rep-qVOkqgk

**23. Inverted Row (Bodyweight)**
Set a barbell in a rack at hip height or use a Smith machine. Lie underneath it and grip the bar with an overhand grip. Pull your chest up to the bar by driving your elbows back, keeping your body straight. A bodyweight row that scales with bar height.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LkDZO7HSQRA

**24. Superman**
Lie face-down on the floor with arms extended overhead. Simultaneously lift your arms, chest, and legs off the floor as high as possible, squeezing your glutes and lower back at the top. Hold briefly, then lower. This bodyweight extension strengthens the entire posterior chain.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cc6UVRS7PW4

**25. Back Extension**
Position yourself on a back extension bench (Roman chair) with your hips at the top of the pad. Lower your torso toward the floor, then raise back to a neutral spine position (or slightly hyperextended). Add weight to a chest plate for progression.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ph3pddpKzzw

**26. Reverse Fly (Dumbbell)**
Hinge forward at the hips until your torso is roughly parallel to the floor. Let the dumbbells hang, then raise both arms out to the sides in a wide arc, squeezing the rear delts and rhomboids at the top. Lower slowly. Keep a slight bend in the elbows throughout.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls5NVSLM0RE

**27. High Row Machine**
Adjust the high row machine seat so the handles are above your head. Pull them down and toward your upper chest in a combined lat pulldown and row movement. This machine effectively targets lats and upper back together with no setup required.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CAwf7n6Luuc

**28. Low Row Machine**
Sit at the low row machine with the handles at lower chest/abdomen height. Pull the handles back and slightly up, squeezing the mid-back at the end range. An extremely safe and progressive option for mid-back development.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GZbfZ033f74

**29. Renegade Row**
Start in a push-up position gripping two dumbbells on the floor. Row one dumbbell to your hip while the other hand and both feet stabilize you. Alternate sides. This challenges anti-rotation core stability alongside back strength — Jeff Nippard notes it's poor for pure hypertrophy but trains functional strength.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOt-dWy2h8Y

**30. Kneeling Cable Row**
Kneel facing a cable machine with the pulley at mid-height. Pull a D-handle to your hip, driving your elbow back past your torso. Kneeling removes lower-body compensation and forces the back to work in isolation.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UCXxvVItLoM

**31. Trap Bar Deadlift**
Stand inside the trap bar (hex bar) with handles on either side. Hinge down to grip the handles, keep your back flat, and drive through your heels to stand upright. The trap bar shifts the load to a more upright position than a conventional deadlift, reducing lower-back stress.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JEBb2q5gXeY

**32. Shrug (Barbell)**
Hold a barbell in front of your thighs with an overhand grip. Shrug your shoulders straight up toward your ears as high as possible, hold briefly, and lower with control. Do not rotate the shoulders — pure vertical movement to target the upper traps.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cJRVVxmytaM

**33. Shrug (Dumbbell)**
Hold a dumbbell in each hand at your sides. Shrug both shoulders straight up simultaneously, pause at the top, then lower slowly. The dumbbells allow a natural shoulder path and can be slightly more comfortable than a barbell for those with wrist issues.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cJRVVxmytaM

### Arms Exercise Descriptions

**1. Barbell Curl**
Stand with feet shoulder-width apart holding a barbell with an underhand grip, hands shoulder-width apart. Curl the bar up to shoulder level by contracting your biceps, keeping your elbows fixed at your sides. Lower slowly to full extension and repeat.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=puSK4XFHr8I

**2. Dumbbell Alternating Curl**
Stand holding a dumbbell in each hand with arms at your sides. Curl one dumbbell up while rotating the palm upward (supination) at the top, squeeze the bicep, then lower it before curling the other arm. Alternating allows a brief rest per side and lets you focus on each arm.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lXwm62SiLQ8

**3. Hammer Curl**
Hold dumbbells with a neutral (hammer) grip — thumbs up — at your sides. Curl both or alternating dumbbells upward, keeping the palms facing each other throughout. This targets the brachialis (underneath the bicep) and brachioradialis, adding overall arm thickness.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lXwm62SiLQ8

**4. Incline Dumbbell Curl**
Set a bench to a 45–60-degree incline and lie back holding dumbbells with your arms hanging behind you. Curl both dumbbells upward — the incline creates a greater stretch on the long head of the bicep at the bottom, making this highly effective for long-head development.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=soxrZlIl35U

**5. Concentration Curl**
Sit on a bench with your legs wide. Rest your elbow against the inside of your thigh and let the dumbbell hang. Curl the dumbbell upward toward your shoulder in a pure bicep movement with no body sway. This is excellent for peak contraction and short-head isolation.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0AUGkch3tzc

**6. Preacher Curl (EZ Bar)**
Rest your upper arms on the angled preacher bench pad and grip an EZ bar with a slightly supinated grip. Curl the bar upward, squeezing at the top, then lower slowly to full extension. The preacher bench eliminates swing and keeps the long head of the bicep under load at the bottom.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fIWP-FRFNU0

**7. Preacher Curl (Machine)**
Sit at the machine preacher curl station and adjust the seat so your upper arms rest fully on the pad. Curl the handle up and squeeze, then lower slowly. Machines provide consistent resistance curves — Jeff Nippard frequently includes machine preacher curls as a top-tier bicep choice.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fIWP-FRFNU0

**8. Cable Curl (Straight Bar)**
Attach a straight bar to a low cable pulley. Stand facing the machine and curl the bar to shoulder height, keeping elbows fixed at your sides. Cable provides constant tension throughout the full range, including the bottom where a dumbbell would have minimal load.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vD-dEl7R2Bg

**9. Cable Curl (Rope)**
Attach a rope to a low cable pulley. Curl the rope upward, splitting the ends at the top to maximize bicep contraction. The rope allows a slightly wider grip and wrist-neutral position during the curl.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vD-dEl7R2Bg

**10. Zottman Curl**
Curl a pair of dumbbells upward with a supinated (underhand) grip, squeeze at the top, then rotate to a pronated (overhand) grip and lower slowly. The pronated lowering phase targets the brachioradialis, making this a two-in-one exercise for complete arm development.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZrFZbgvG6T4

**11. EZ Bar Curl**
Grip an EZ bar at the angled inner grips (roughly shoulder-width). Curl the bar to chest level, keeping elbows stable. The angled grip reduces supination stress on the wrists compared to a straight barbell — good option if straight bar curls cause wrist discomfort.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=puSK4XFHr8I

**12. Cross-Body Hammer Curl**
Hold a dumbbell in one hand and curl it across your body toward the opposite shoulder rather than straight up. This targets the brachialis and long head of the bicep from a slightly different angle than a standard hammer curl.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lXwm62SiLQ8

**13. Spider Curl**
Lie prone (face-down) on an incline bench and let the dumbbells hang below you. Curl both dumbbells up, focusing on contracting the biceps. The prone position prevents any body lean compensation and is one of the best exercises for short-head peak contraction.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=soxrZlIl35U

**14. Reverse Curl**
Hold a barbell or dumbbells with a pronated (overhand) grip. Curl the weight upward while maintaining the overhand grip position. This targets the brachioradialis and forearms heavily, and is an often-neglected movement for complete arm thickness.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nMcwDECyeP4

**15. Cable Hammer Curl (Rope)**
Attach a rope to a low cable pulley. Hold the rope with a neutral grip (thumbs up) and curl upward, pulling the rope slightly apart at the top of the movement. Constant cable tension throughout adds stimulus particularly at the bottom of the curl.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vD-dEl7R2Bg

**16. Chin-Up (Biceps Focus)**
Perform a chin-up with an underhand shoulder-width grip but consciously focus on your biceps driving the movement. At the top, squeeze your biceps and hold briefly. Lower with a slow, controlled 3-count. Chin-ups are one of the heaviest bicep loading exercises possible.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=brhRXlOhsAM

**17. Overhead Tricep Extension (Cable)**
Attach a rope to a high cable pulley and face away from the machine. Hold the rope behind your head and extend your arms overhead to lockout, squeezing the triceps. Return slowly. The overhead position puts the long head of the tricep in a fully lengthened position — the top-ranked tricep exercise by evidence.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AMn59IKSgRw

**18. Overhead Tricep Extension (Dumbbell, One-Arm)**
Sit or stand and hold a dumbbell with one hand directly above your head. Lower it behind your head by bending the elbow, then press back to lockout. Keep your upper arm close to your ear throughout. The overhead position stretches the long head of the tricep maximally.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k8mkr459wA8

**19. Overhead Tricep Extension (EZ Bar)**
Sit on a bench holding an EZ bar with both hands above your head, grip at the inner angles. Lower the bar behind your head by bending at the elbows, then press back overhead. The EZ bar reduces wrist strain compared to a straight bar.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AMn59IKSgRw

**20. Tricep Pushdown (Straight Bar)**
Attach a straight bar to a high cable pulley. Stand facing the machine and push the bar down to full arm extension while keeping your elbows tucked at your sides. Squeeze the triceps at the bottom, then allow the bar to rise back up slowly. This primarily targets the lateral head.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2-LAMcpzODU

**21. Tricep Pushdown (Rope)**
Attach a rope to a high cable. Perform pushdowns and at the bottom of the movement pull the rope ends apart to maximize the contraction. The rope allows a more natural wrist position than a straight bar and provides a slightly wider range at the bottom.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2-LAMcpzODU

**22. Skull Crusher (EZ Bar)**
Lie flat on a bench holding an EZ bar at arms' length above your chest. Lower the bar to your forehead (or behind your head for more long-head stretch) by bending only at the elbows. Press back up to full extension. One of the most effective exercises for overall tricep mass.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ir5PsbniVSc

**23. Skull Crusher (Dumbbell)**
Lie flat holding a dumbbell in each hand above your chest. Lower both dumbbells to either side of your head, bending at the elbows only. Press back up together. Dumbbells allow each arm to work independently and can be more comfortable for some wrist positions.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ir5PsbniVSc

**24. Close-Grip Bench Press**
Lie on a bench and grip the barbell with hands shoulder-width apart or slightly narrower. Lower the bar to your sternum keeping elbows tucked close to your body, then press up to lockout. Emphasizes triceps over chest compared to a standard-grip bench press.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nEF0bv2FW94

**25. Tricep Dips (Bench)**
Sit on the edge of a bench with your hands gripping the edge beside your hips. Slide forward and lower yourself off the bench by bending your elbows to roughly 90 degrees. Press back up. Beginner-friendly tricep dip variation — add weight on the lap for progression.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0326dy_-CzM

**26. Tricep Dips (Parallel Bars)**
Grip parallel bars and support yourself at full arm extension. Lower your body by bending your elbows, keeping your torso upright and elbows close to your body, until your elbows reach 90 degrees. Press back up. One of the strongest bodyweight tricep exercises.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2z8JmcrW-As

**27. Diamond Push-Up**
Place your hands together directly under your chest forming a diamond shape. Lower your chest toward your hands, keeping elbows tucked close to your body. Press back up. This variation maximally loads the triceps in a bodyweight context.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J0DXDExNScE

**28. Cable Kickback**
Attach a D-handle to a low cable and face the machine in a slight hinge position. Start with your upper arm parallel to the floor and extend your forearm back until your arm is fully straight. Squeeze the tricep at full extension and return slowly.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SS6K3lAwZ8

**29. Dumbbell Kickback**
Hinge forward at the hips and place one knee and hand on a bench for support. Hold a dumbbell in your free hand with your upper arm parallel to the floor. Extend your arm fully behind you, squeezing the tricep, then slowly return. Good for isolation but limited loading compared to pressing/extension movements.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SS6K3lAwZ8

**30. Tate Press**
Lie flat on a bench holding two dumbbells above your chest with elbows flared wide. Lower the dumbbells inward and downward toward your sternum by bending your elbows out to the sides, then press back up. This unique motion strongly targets the lateral and medial tricep heads.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X3skCb3WNx8

**31. JM Press**
Lie on a bench and grip a barbell like a close-grip bench press. Bring the bar down toward your throat in a partial motion that sits between a skull crusher and a close-grip press. It is a hybrid movement that overloads the tricep in a compound pressing context. Best for intermediate-advanced lifters.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nEF0bv2FW94

**32. Single-Arm Cable Overhead Extension**
Attach one handle to a high cable pulley and face away from the machine. Hold the handle behind your head with one arm and extend upward to lockout. Single-arm work increases the range of motion and isolates each tricep head independently.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AMn59IKSgRw

**33. Supinated Cable Curl**
Attach a straight bar to a low cable. Curl it upward with your palms facing up throughout the entire movement — focus on maintaining full supination at the top to maximize bicep contraction. The cable provides constant tension at the bottom where free weights unload.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vD-dEl7R2Bg

**34. Machine Bicep Curl**
Adjust the machine seat so your upper arms rest on the pad and grip the handles. Curl up to full contraction and hold briefly, then lower to full extension. The machine guides the movement path and is an excellent controlled option, especially as a finisher.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fIWP-FRFNU0

### Shoulder Exercise Descriptions

**1. Barbell Overhead Press**
Stand with the bar resting on your upper chest and press it vertically overhead to full lockout. As the bar passes your head, lean your head back slightly to let it pass, then re-establish a straight bar path. Lower with control to the clavicle area. Bracing your core throughout protects the lower back.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SgyUoY0IZ7A

**2. Dumbbell Shoulder Press (Seated)**
Sit on a bench with back support holding dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing forward. Press both dumbbells overhead to near lockout, then lower back to shoulder height. Seated position allows heavier loads with reduced stabilization demands.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SgyUoY0IZ7A

**3. Dumbbell Shoulder Press (Standing)**
Stand holding dumbbells at shoulder height. Press both overhead while bracing your core to prevent lumbar hyperextension. Standing recruits additional stabilizing muscles compared to seated and is more functional. Lower controlled to starting position.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SgyUoY0IZ7A

**4. Machine Shoulder Press**
Adjust the seat height so the handles are at shoulder level. Press up to full extension and lower with control. Jeff Nippard ranks this as his personal favorite shoulder exercise — the fixed path allows deep concentration on delt contraction without balance demands.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SgyUoY0IZ7A

**5. Arnold Press**
Sit holding dumbbells at shoulder height with palms facing you. As you press upward, rotate your palms outward so they face forward at the top. Reverse on the way down. The rotation recruits all three deltoid heads across the range of motion.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3ml7BH7mNwQ

**6. Landmine Press (Shoulder)**
Hold the sleeve of a landmine barbell at shoulder level with one or both hands. Press the bar up and forward in an arc. The landmine angle is very shoulder-friendly and creates strong upper pec and anterior delt activation with a natural arc of movement.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ql7FEA9LWfM

**7. Dumbbell Lateral Raise**
Stand holding dumbbells at your sides. Raise both arms out to the side to shoulder height, leading with the elbows slightly, then lower slowly. Slight forward lean of the torso and a soft elbow bend improves delt tension. This is the foundational lateral delt exercise.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SgyUoY0IZ7A

**8. Cable Lateral Raise**
Attach a D-handle to a low cable pulley. Stand side-on to the machine and raise your outside arm to shoulder height, then lower slowly. The cable provides constant tension throughout including at the bottom where a dumbbell has no load. A 2025 Frontiers in Physiology study found cables equally effective as dumbbells for lateral delt hypertrophy.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SgyUoY0IZ7A

**9. Machine Lateral Raise**
Sit at the lateral raise machine with pads against your forearms. Raise both arms against the pads to shoulder height, squeeze the lateral delts, and lower slowly. Machines provide very consistent resistance and eliminate the need for balance — excellent for high reps and drop sets.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SgyUoY0IZ7A

**10. Lean-Away Dumbbell Lateral Raise**
Hold a fixed object with one hand for support and lean away from it. Hold a dumbbell in the free hand and perform a lateral raise. The lean increases the stretch on the delt at the bottom and shifts the resistance curve — Jeff Nippard identifies this as the most effective dumbbell lateral raise variation.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=poHGKeLAIaM

**11. Cable Cross-Body Y-Raise**
Set a low cable pulley on one side. Cross your arm across your body to grab the handle, then raise it up and out in a Y-shape away from the machine. Jeff Nippard identifies this as his go-to for lateral delts — crossing the body creates a tremendous initial stretch before the raise.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=poHGKeLAIaM

**12. Plate Front Raise**
Hold a weight plate in front of your thighs with both hands at 9 and 3 o'clock. Raise the plate to shoulder height or slightly above, then lower slowly. The plate forces both arms to work simultaneously and is easy to implement as a warm-up or burnout movement.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbWfA_s0XL8

**13. Dumbbell Front Raise**
Hold a dumbbell in each hand in front of your thighs. Raise one or both dumbbells to shoulder height in front of you, keeping a slight elbow bend. Lower slowly. While effective for anterior delt isolation, the front delt already receives significant work from pressing — prioritize lateral and rear delt work.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbWfA_s0XL8

**14. Cable Front Raise**
Attach a straight bar or single handle to a low cable. Face away from the machine and raise the handle to shoulder height. Cable maintains tension even at the lowest point of the raise where a dumbbell would have almost none.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbWfA_s0XL8

**15. Reverse Pec Deck Fly**

YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rep-qVOkqgk

**16. Dumbbell Reverse Fly**

YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls5NVSLM0RE

**17. Cable Rear Delt Fly**

**18. Face Pull**

**20. Band Pull-Apart**

**21. Seated Dumbbell Lateral Raise**

**23. Upright Row (Cable)**

**25. Pike Push-Up**

YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbWfA_s0XL8

**26. Handstand Push-Up**
Kick into a handstand against a wall with arms fully extended. Lower your head toward the floor under control by bending your elbows, then press back up. This advanced movement maximally loads the overhead press pattern using your full bodyweight. Only attempt if you are confident with wall-supported handstands.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FbWfA_s0XL8

**27. Dumbbell Cuban Press**
Hold dumbbells at your sides. Perform an upright row to chin height, then externally rotate your arms so dumbbells point upward, then press overhead, and reverse the motion. This combination movement trains lateral delt, rear delt, and rotator cuff together.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3ml7BH7mNwQ

**28. Seated Bent-Over Rear Delt Raise**
Sit at the end of a bench and hinge forward until your chest nearly touches your thighs. Hold dumbbells under your knees and raise both arms out to the sides, squeezing the rear delts. The seated hinge position produces a stronger rear delt stretch than the standing version.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls5NVSLM0RE

**29. Single-Arm Cable Rear Delt Fly**
Set a cable to shoulder height and stand side-on to the machine. Using your far hand, pull the handle across your body in a rear fly motion, leading with the elbow. Single-arm work allows you to focus on each rear delt independently and achieve a greater range.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rep-qVOkqgk

**30. Trap Bar Shrug**
Stand inside a trap bar. Grasp the handles and shrug your shoulders straight upward as high as possible, pausing at the top. The neutral grip of a trap bar can be more comfortable than a barbell for shrugs and allows you to hold more weight than dumbbells.

**31. Cable Upright Row**

YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amLSSb8cXok

**32. W-Raise (Dumbbell)**
Lie face-down on a flat or slight incline bench. Hold light dumbbells and raise your arms to form a W shape (elbows bent, hands facing forward, elbows at shoulder height). Hold and lower. This trains the rear delts and external rotators in a combined movement — important for shoulder health and posture.

### Abs Exercise Descriptions

**1. Crunch**
Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Curl your upper back off the floor by contracting your abs, lifting your shoulder blades clear of the ground. Hold briefly at the top, then lower slowly without fully relaxing between reps.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1G0y8D5rFDc

**2. Bicycle Crunch**
Lie on your back with hands behind your head and legs raised. Bring your left knee toward your chest while rotating your right elbow to meet it, then alternate sides in a pedaling motion. Keep the movement controlled — quality of rotation matters more than speed.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1G0y8D5rFDc

**3. Reverse Crunch**
Lie flat with arms at your sides and legs raised to 90 degrees. Pull your knees toward your chest by curling your pelvis off the floor, squeezing the lower abs at the top. Lower your hips back down slowly. This emphasizes the lower rectus abdominis more than a standard crunch.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1G0y8D5rFDc

**4. Leg Raise (Lying)**
Lie flat on your back with legs straight and hands under your glutes for lumbar support. Raise both legs to 90 degrees, then lower them slowly without letting your heels touch the floor. Lower abs and hip flexors work together — keep your lower back in contact with the floor throughout.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JB2oyawG9KI

**5. Hanging Leg Raise**
Hang from a pull-up bar with an overhand grip. Raise both legs (straight or bent) until your thighs are parallel to the floor or higher. Lower slowly without swinging. This is one of the strongest EMG-producing lower ab exercises and also builds grip strength.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hdng3Nm1x_E

**6. Hanging Knee Raise**
Hang from a pull-up bar. Bring your knees up toward your chest, rounding your lower back to tuck the pelvis. This is an easier regression from the hanging leg raise while still delivering strong lower ab activation.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hdng3Nm1x_E

**7. Captain's Chair Leg Raise**
Position yourself in the captain's chair with your forearms on the pads and body hanging freely. Raise your knees toward your chest or, for more difficulty, keep legs straight. Lower slowly. The padded support removes grip fatigue that limits hanging versions.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hdng3Nm1x_E

**8. Ab Wheel Rollout**
Kneel on the floor and hold an ab wheel in front of you. Roll the wheel forward slowly until your hips are nearly on the floor and your body is fully extended. Pull the wheel back by contracting your abs hard. One of the highest-rated exercises for overall core activation.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1G0y8D5rFDc

**9. Plank**
Support yourself on your forearms and toes, body in a straight line from head to heels. Hold this position while breathing normally. Brace your abs, squeeze your glutes, and avoid letting your hips sag or rise. Progress by increasing hold duration or adding weight to your back.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B296mZDhrP4

**10. Side Plank**
Lie on your side and push up onto one forearm and the side of your foot. Keep your body in a straight line from head to feet, hips lifted. Hold and then switch sides. This targets the quadratus lumborum and obliques — structures critical for spinal lateral stability.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B296mZDhrP4

**11. RKC Plank**
Hold a standard plank position but forcefully pull your elbows toward your feet and your feet toward your elbows simultaneously — creating total-body tension. This dramatically increases core activation compared to a passive plank hold. A 20-second RKC plank may be harder than a 2-minute standard plank.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B296mZDhrP4

**12. Hollow Body Hold**
Lie on your back and press your lower back into the floor. Raise your arms overhead and legs off the floor, keeping both as straight as possible. Maintain a full lower-back contact with the floor throughout. This gymnastics staple creates extreme anterior core tension and is foundational for advanced movements.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44ScXWFaVBs

**13. Dead Bug**
Lie on your back and raise both arms straight up and both legs to 90 degrees. Slowly lower your right arm and left leg simultaneously until nearly touching the floor, then return. Alternate sides. The contralateral movement demands high core stability and anti-extension control.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44ScXWFaVBs

**14. Bird Dog**
Start in a quadruped position (all-fours). Extend your right arm and left leg simultaneously, hold briefly, return, and alternate sides. Keep your back flat and hips level throughout — avoid rotation. This trains deep core stabilizers (transverse abdominis and multifidus) essential for spinal health.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44ScXWFaVBs

**15. Russian Twist**
Sit with knees bent and feet hovering slightly off the floor. Lean back to 45 degrees. Rotate your torso side to side, touching the floor on each side. Keep your chest up and avoid rounding the back. A classic oblique movement — add a weight plate or medicine ball for progression.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wkD8rjkodUI

**16. Weighted Russian Twist**
Perform a Russian Twist holding a dumbbell, weight plate, or medicine ball. The added load significantly increases oblique demand. Control the rotation speed — slower eccentrics produce greater muscular tension.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wkD8rjkodUI

**17. Cable Crunch**
Kneel in front of a high cable pulley holding a rope attachment behind your head. Crunch down by rounding your upper back toward your knees, bringing your elbows toward your thighs. The cable provides constant load throughout the crunch — allowing heavier progressive overload than bodyweight crunches.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cgOHhj7TLUQ

**18. Decline Sit-Up**
Secure your feet under the pads of a decline bench. Lower your upper body toward the bench (not fully down), then sit up to vertical. The decline angle increases the range of motion and loads the rectus abdominis harder than a flat sit-up.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1G0y8D5rFDc

**19. V-Up**
Lie flat on your back with arms extended overhead. Simultaneously raise your legs and torso, reaching your hands toward your toes to form a V shape. Lower everything back to the floor slowly. This combines upper and lower ab flexion in one movement.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iP2fjvG0g3c

**20. L-Sit Hold**
Support yourself between parallel bars (or on the floor with push-up handles) with arms fully extended. Raise your legs to parallel with the floor and hold. This requires tremendous hip flexor and core strength — begin with tucked legs and progress to full L-sit.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IUZJoSP66HI

**21. Pallof Press**
Set a cable at chest height and stand perpendicular to the machine. Hold the handle with both hands at your chest, then press it straight out in front of you and hold for 1–2 seconds before returning. The anti-rotation tension required to prevent your torso from twisting is the training stimulus.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AH_QZLm_0-s

**22. Landmine Rotation**
Hold the end of a landmine barbell with both hands in front of your chest. Rotate the bar from side to side in a wide arc at hip/chest height while keeping your hips facing forward. The load challenges rotational control of the core — similar to a wood chop but with a fixed pivot point.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AH_QZLm_0-s

**23. Wood Chop (Cable)**
Set a cable to the high position on one side. Hold the handle with both hands, then pull it diagonally downward across your body toward the opposite hip in a chopping motion. Pivot your back foot to allow hip rotation. This trains rotational power and oblique strength.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AH_QZLm_0-s

**24. Mountain Climber**
Start in a high plank. Rapidly bring one knee toward your chest, then return and alternate legs — as if running in place from a push-up position. Engage your core hard to prevent hip rotation. This is both a core stability exercise and a cardiovascular challenge.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nmwgirgXLYM

**25. Flutter Kick**
Lie flat on your back with hands under your glutes and legs slightly off the floor. Kick your legs alternately up and down in a small range flutter motion, keeping your core braced and lower back pressed to the floor. Great for lower ab endurance.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JB2oyawG9KI

**26. Scissor Kick**
Lie on your back with legs raised to 45 degrees. Cross your legs over each other in alternating scissor fashion. Keep the lower back pressed to the floor and the core tight throughout. Similar to flutter kicks but with a lateral crossing motion.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JB2oyawG9KI

**27. Toe Touch Crunch**
Lie on your back with legs straight and raised to 90 degrees. Crunch upward and reach both hands toward your toes. Lower slowly without fully resting your shoulder blades between reps. The raised legs eliminate hip flexor dominance and force the rectus abdominis to do the work.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1G0y8D5rFDc

**28. Oblique Crunch**
Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat. Drop both knees to one side so your lower body is twisted. Crunch straight upward with your upper body against the twist of your lower body. This creates strong oblique engagement on the upward side.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wkD8rjkodUI

**29. Dragon Flag**
Lie on a bench and grip a fixed object above your head for support. Raise your entire body off the bench to a vertical position supported only by your upper back. Lower your body toward the bench slowly while keeping it perfectly straight — do not pike. This is one of the most demanding full-core exercises.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IUZJoSP66HI

**30. Toes-to-Bar**
Hang from a pull-up bar and raise your legs with straight knees until your toes touch the bar. Lower slowly under control. This requires significant lat and hip flexor contribution alongside extreme core strength — a full-body movement dominated by the lower abs.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hdng3Nm1x_E

**31. Seated Knee Tuck**
Sit on the edge of a bench with your hands gripping the sides for support. Lean slightly back and extend your legs out, then pull your knees into your chest. Extend again without fully placing your feet on the floor. A beginner-friendly alternative to hanging knee raises.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JB2oyawG9KI

**32. Stability Ball Crunch**
Sit on a stability ball and walk your feet forward until your lower back is supported by the ball. Perform a crunch by curling your upper back forward and upward. The ball increases the range of motion compared to a floor crunch by allowing your back to arch over the ball first.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1G0y8D5rFDc

**33. Ab Rollout (Barbell)**
Load a barbell with small round plates and kneel behind it. Roll the bar forward while keeping your body straight, extending your hips down toward the floor, then pull the bar back. More challenging than the ab wheel rollout — only use if ab wheel rollout is comfortable.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1G0y8D5rFDc

**34. Suitcase Carry**
Hold a heavy dumbbell or kettlebell in one hand at your side. Walk for a set distance or time while keeping your torso completely upright — resist leaning toward the weight. This trains lateral core stability (quadratus lumborum) in a highly functional loaded carry pattern.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AH_QZLm_0-s

### Legs Exercise Descriptions

**1. Barbell Back Squat**
Position a barbell across your upper traps (high bar) or lower traps (low bar). With feet shoulder-width apart and toes slightly out, descend by hinging at the hips and bending the knees until your thighs are parallel to the floor or below. Drive through your heels to stand back up. This is the foundational lower-body compound movement.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gcNh17Ckjgg

**2. Barbell Front Squat**
Hold the barbell across your front deltoids in a clean rack position with elbows high. Descend with an upright torso to parallel or below, keeping your elbows from dropping. Drive up to lockout. The front-loaded position demands greater quad activation and core strength than the back squat.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gcNh17Ckjgg

**3. Goblet Squat**
Hold a single dumbbell vertically at chest level with both hands. Feet shoulder-width apart with toes turned slightly out. Squat down between your legs as deep as possible, using the weight as a counterbalance. Drive up to stand. This is an excellent beginner squat pattern teacher.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gcNh17Ckjgg

**4. Leg Press**
Sit in the leg press machine with feet shoulder-width apart on the platform. Release the safety handles and lower the platform toward your chest by bending your knees to 90 degrees. Press back to near lockout (keep a slight knee bend at the top). A safe way to load the quads and glutes without spinal loading.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y4Vv2ASsyhs

**5. Hack Squat (Machine)**
Step into the hack squat machine with your back flat against the pad and feet on the platform. Lower the weight until your knees reach or pass 90 degrees, then drive back up. The machine angle places the load directly on the quads with significantly less lower-back involvement than a barbell squat.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y4Vv2ASsyhs

**6. Leg Extension**
Sit in the leg extension machine with the pad against your lower shins. Extend your knees fully, squeezing the quads hard at the top, then lower slowly. A pure quad isolation exercise — research shows the extended position (fully straightened leg) provides a strong stimulus to the rectus femoris.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YyvSfVjQeL0

**7. Bulgarian Split Squat**
Stand facing away from a bench and rest one foot on it behind you. Hold dumbbells at your sides and descend by bending your front knee until your back knee nearly touches the floor. Drive up through your front heel. One of the most demanding single-leg exercises — highly effective for quads and glutes.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sXVbOBFPig

**8. Lunge (Walking)**
Stand upright and step forward with one foot into a lunge position — front knee at 90 degrees, back knee just above the floor. Drive through the front heel to bring the back foot forward into the next lunge step. Keep your torso upright throughout.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L8fvypPrv3Y

**9. Lunge (Dumbbell)**
Hold a dumbbell in each hand at your sides. Step forward into a lunge, lower your back knee toward the floor, then push back to standing. Complete all reps on one side before switching, or alternate legs. The dumbbells increase the loading demand significantly.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L8fvypPrv3Y

**10. Reverse Lunge**
Stand upright and step backward with one foot into a lunge position. Your front knee stays over your front foot while the back knee lowers. Drive through the front heel to return to standing. Reverse lunges are kinder to the knee than forward lunges and are easier to balance for most people.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L8fvypPrv3Y

**11. Step-Up**
Stand in front of a bench or box. Step up with one foot and drive through that heel to lift your entire body onto the box, then step back down. According to the Journal of Sports Science Medicine, the step-up produces the highest glute activation of any lower-body exercise tested. Keep the torso upright throughout.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GgW5EtBrCuE

**12. Step-Up with Dumbbells**
Hold dumbbells at your sides and perform step-ups onto a bench or box. The added weight increases glute and quad loading significantly. Keep your torso upright and avoid pushing off the trailing foot to ensure full working leg activation.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GgW5EtBrCuE

**13. Sissy Squat**
Stand with heels elevated on a small plate or wedge and place your hands on a support. Lean your torso back as you descend by bending your knees forward, keeping your hips extended — your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders at the bottom. This places the rectus femoris in full stretch under load.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sXVbOBFPig

**14. Heel-Elevated Goblet Squat**
Place your heels on a 1–2-inch wedge or plates and hold a dumbbell at your chest. Squat as deep as possible — the heel elevation allows a more upright torso and dramatically increases quad recruitment compared to flat-footed squats. Excellent for quad-focused training.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gcNh17Ckjgg

**15. Romanian Deadlift (Barbell)**
Hold a barbell in front of your thighs. Hinge at the hips by pushing them back while lowering the bar along your legs until you feel a strong hamstring stretch (typically just below the knees). Drive your hips forward to stand. Keep your back flat and the bar close to your body throughout.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_oyxCn2iSjU

**16. Romanian Deadlift (Dumbbell)**
Hold dumbbells in front of your thighs and perform the same hip-hinge pattern as the barbell RDL. Dumbbells allow the weight to travel slightly further from the body which can increase the hamstring stretch. Excellent for those learning the hip hinge pattern.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQgFixeXdZo

**17. Conventional Deadlift**
Stand with feet hip-width apart and a barbell over mid-foot. Grip the bar just outside your legs, set a flat back, and drive through the floor to lift the bar to hip lockout. Lower with control. This is the king of posterior chain exercises, loading the hamstrings, glutes, lower back, and traps simultaneously.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=op9kVnSso6Q

**18. Sumo Deadlift**
Take a wide stance with toes turned out significantly and grip the bar with a narrower grip inside your legs. Descend and pull using the same principles as a conventional deadlift — flat back, bar close to the body. The wider stance recruits the inner thighs and glutes more than conventional stance.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=op9kVnSso6Q

**19. Lying Leg Curl**
Lie face-down on the leg curl machine with the pad against your lower calves. Curl your heels toward your glutes by bending at the knees, squeeze at the top, then lower slowly. This isolates the hamstrings in a shortened position at the hip — combine with RDLs for complete hamstring development.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0a_fVS2s4Ho

**20. Seated Leg Curl**
Sit at the seated leg curl machine with the back pad supporting your upper back and the lower pad across your calves. Curl your heels downward, squeeze at the bottom, and return slowly. Research indicates seated leg curls better isolate the hamstrings at a stretched hip position than lying curls.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0a_fVS2s4Ho

**21. Nordic Hamstring Curl**
Kneel on a mat with your ankles secured under something fixed. Lower your torso toward the floor as slowly as possible while keeping your body rigid, resisting the fall with your hamstrings. Catch yourself with your hands and push back up. One of the strongest hamstring eccentric loading exercises.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0a_fVS2s4Ho

**22. Good Morning**
Place a barbell across your upper back as for a squat. Hinge forward at the hips with a slight knee bend, lowering your torso until roughly parallel to the floor, then drive your hips forward to return to standing. This directly loads the hamstrings and lower back in the lengthened position.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YA-h3n9L4YU

**23. Hip Thrust (Barbell)**
Sit on the floor with your upper back against a bench, a barbell resting across your hip crease. Drive through your heels to thrust your hips upward until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze your glutes hard at the top and lower slowly. The most effective exercise for glute hypertrophy.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GgW5EtBrCuE

**24. Hip Thrust (Dumbbell)**
Set up the same as a barbell hip thrust but hold a single dumbbell vertically over your hip crease. This is an accessible version that is easier to set up and ideal for learning the movement pattern before loading a barbell.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GgW5EtBrCuE

**25. Glute Bridge**
Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Drive your hips upward by contracting your glutes until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders. Lower your hips slowly without touching the floor before the next rep. A foundational glute activation exercise requiring no equipment.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wPM8icPu6H8

**26. Cable Kickback (Glute)**
Attach an ankle cuff to a low cable pulley. Face the machine and hold it for balance. Kick the attached leg straight back as far as possible by extending the hip, squeezing the glute hard at the top. Lower with control. An effective isolation movement for the glutes.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wPM8icPu6H8

**27. Donkey Kick**
Start on all fours. Keep one knee bent at 90 degrees and kick that foot toward the ceiling by extending the hip, squeezing the glute at the top. Lower the knee back without touching the floor and repeat. A simple bodyweight glute isolation exercise with no equipment needed.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wPM8icPu6H8

**28. Sumo Squat (Dumbbell)**
Hold a single heavy dumbbell vertically with both hands in front of you. Take a wide stance with toes turned out approximately 45 degrees. Squat deep between your legs, keeping your torso upright, then drive up to standing. This targets the inner thighs and glutes more than a standard squat.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gcNh17Ckjgg

**29. Standing Calf Raise**
Stand on a calf raise machine or a step with the balls of your feet on the edge and heels hanging. Rise up onto your tiptoes as high as possible, hold briefly, then lower your heels below the step level for a full stretch. Use a slow pace (3 seconds up, 3 seconds down) for maximum gastrocnemius stimulus.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gwLzBJYoWlI

**30. Seated Calf Raise**
Sit at the seated calf raise machine with the pad across your lower thighs. Raise your heels as high as possible, pause, then lower below the step level for a full stretch. The seated position (knees bent) minimizes gastrocnemius contribution and isolates the soleus — the deeper calf muscle.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gwLzBJYoWlI

**31. Single-Leg Calf Raise**
Stand on one foot on a step or raised surface with the ball of your foot on the edge. Hold a wall or rack for balance. Rise onto your tiptoe, hold, then lower slowly for a full stretch. Single-leg loading approximately doubles the calf stimulus compared to bilateral raises.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gwLzBJYoWlI

**32. Leg Press Calf Raise**
Sit in the leg press machine with your legs mostly extended and only the balls of your feet on the lower edge of the platform. Push the platform away by plantarflexing your ankles (rising onto your tiptoes), then lower to a full ankle stretch. A convenient calf isolation option using machine support.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gwLzBJYoWlI

**33. Box Jump**
Stand facing a sturdy box. Lower into a brief quarter squat, swing your arms, and jump explosively upward, landing softly on top of the box with both feet simultaneously and knees slightly bent. Step down (do not jump down) and reset. Trains explosive power in the quads, glutes, and calves.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=52r-M5-dn40

**34. Lateral Lunge**
Stand upright with feet together. Step one foot wide to the side and bend that knee into a deep lateral lunge, pushing your hips back and keeping the opposite leg straight. Push back to the starting position and alternate sides. This targets the quads, glutes, and inner thighs through a lateral plane of motion.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L8fvypPrv3Y

**35. Curtsy Lunge**
Stand and step one foot behind and across the other in a curtsy motion. Bend both knees and lower your back knee toward the floor, then drive back up. This targets the glutes and abductors from a unique diagonal angle and is highly effective for glute medius activation.
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L8fvypPrv3Y
